## Exam Overview

### Exam Format

- **Type:** Multiple choice
- **Questions:** 75 questions
- **Duration:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Passing Score:** 49 out of 75 (65%)
- **Language:** English (and other languages)
- **Format:** Closed book

### Exam Topics

The exam covers:

1. **COBIT Framework Introduction**
   - What COBIT is
   - Purpose and benefits
   - COBIT 2019 vs COBIT 5

2. **Governance vs Management**
   - Difference between governance and management
   - Governance activities
   - Management activities

3. **Core Model**
   - 40 governance and management objectives
   - Five domains
   - Objective structure

4. **Design Factors**
   - 11 design factors
   - How to use design factors
   - Tailoring the framework

5. **Implementation**
   - Implementation approach
   - Focus areas
   - Maturity models

## Key Topics to Study

### 1. COBIT Framework Introduction

**Must Know:**
- What COBIT 2019 is
- Purpose of COBIT
- Key characteristics
- COBIT 2019 vs COBIT 5 changes

**Practice Questions:**
- What is COBIT 2019?
- What is the purpose of COBIT?
- What are key changes from COBIT 5?

### 2. Governance vs Management

**Must Know:**
- Difference between governance and management
- Governance activities (Evaluate, Direct, Monitor)
- Management activities (Plan, Build, Run, Monitor)
- EDM domain vs other domains

**Practice Questions:**
- What is the difference between governance and management?
- What are governance activities?
- Which domain is governance?

### 3. Core Model

**Must Know:**
- 40 objectives total
- Five domains (EDM, APO, BAI, DSS, MEA)
- Number of objectives in each domain
- Objective structure

**Practice Questions:**
- How many objectives are in COBIT 2019?
- What are the five domains?
- How many objectives in EDM domain?

### 4. Design Factors

**Must Know:**
- 11 design factors
- What each factor influences
- How to use design factors
- Tailoring the framework

**Practice Questions:**
- How many design factors are there?
- Name five design factors
- How do design factors influence framework design?

### 5. Implementation

**Must Know:**
- Implementation approach
- Focus areas
- Maturity models
- Governance system components

**Practice Questions:**
- What is the implementation approach?
- What are focus areas?
- What are governance system components?

## Study Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

1. **Read Official Materials**
   - COBIT 2019 Framework: Introduction and Methodology
   - Study all foundation materials

2. **Understand Core Concepts**
   - Governance vs management
   - 40 objectives
   - Five domains
   - Design factors

3. **Take Notes**
   - Write down key concepts
   - Create summaries
   - Make flashcards

### Phase 2: Deep Dive (Weeks 3-4)

1. **Study Objectives**
   - Understand each domain
   - Learn key objectives
   - Understand relationships

2. **Practice Questions**
   - Use practice exams
   - Identify weak areas
   - Review incorrect answers

3. **Review and Reinforce**
   - Review notes regularly
   - Practice with flashcards
   - Discuss with others

### Phase 3: Exam Prep (Week 5)

1. **Mock Exams**
   - Take full practice exams
   - Time yourself
   - Review all answers

2. **Final Review**
   - Review weak areas
   - Review key concepts
   - Review exam format

3. **Exam Day Prep**
   - Get good sleep
   - Arrive early
   - Bring required documents

## Practice Exam Tips

### Reading Questions

1. **Read Carefully**
   - Read entire question
   - Understand what's being asked
   - Watch for negative words

2. **Identify Key Words**
   - Look for COBIT-specific terms
   - Identify the topic
   - Note the question type

### Answering Questions

1. **Eliminate Wrong Answers**
   - Cross out obviously wrong answers
   - Narrow down choices
   - Use process of elimination

2. **Look for Best Answer**
   - Multiple answers may seem correct
   - Choose the BEST answer
   - Consider COBIT principles

3. **Don't Overthink**
   - Trust your knowledge
   - Don't second-guess
   - Answer and move on

### Time Management

1. **Pace Yourself**
   - 120 minutes for 75 questions
   - ~1.6 minutes per question
   - Don't spend too long on one question

2. **Flag Difficult Questions**
   - Answer if you know it
   - Flag if unsure
   - Return to flagged questions

3. **Review Time**
   - Leave time for review
   - Check flagged questions
   - Verify answers
