## What are Design Factors?

**Design Factors** are factors that influence how the COBIT framework should be designed and tailored for a specific organization.

### Purpose

Design factors help organizations:
- **Tailor COBIT** to their specific context
- **Focus** on relevant objectives
- **Prioritize** implementation
- **Customize** the framework

---

## The 11 Design Factors

### 1. Enterprise Strategy

**Description:**  
The organization's overall strategy and direction.

**Influence:**
- Which objectives are most important
- How IT supports strategy
- Strategic priorities

**Example:**  
If strategy is digital transformation → Focus on innovation, agility objectives.

---

### 2. Enterprise Goals

**Description:**  
Specific goals the organization wants to achieve.

**Influence:**
- Which objectives support goals
- Priority of objectives
- Success metrics

**Example:**  
If goal is cost reduction → Focus on cost management objectives.

---

### 3. Risk Profile

**Description:**  
The organization's risk appetite and risk profile.

**Influence:**
- Risk management focus
- Security objectives priority
- Compliance requirements

**Example:**  
High-risk profile → Strong focus on risk and security objectives.

---

### 4. I&T-Related Issues

**Description:**  
Current IT-related challenges and issues.

**Influence:**
- Which objectives address issues
- Implementation priorities
- Quick wins

**Example:**  
Issue: Poor change management → Focus on change management objectives.

---

### 5. Threat Landscape

**Description:**  
Current and emerging threats to the organization.

**Influence:**
- Security objectives priority
- Risk management focus
- Threat response capabilities

**Example:**  
High cybersecurity threats → Strong focus on security objectives.

---

### 6. Compliance Requirements

**Description:**  
Regulatory and compliance requirements.

**Influence:**
- Compliance objectives priority
- Audit requirements
- Control requirements

**Example:**  
Banking industry → Strong compliance focus, regulatory requirements.

---

### 7. Role of IT

**Description:**  
How IT supports the business.

**Influence:**
- IT objectives priority
- IT governance focus
- IT-business alignment

**Example:**  
IT as strategic enabler → Focus on strategic IT objectives.

---

### 8. Sourcing Model

**Description:**  
How IT services are sourced (internal, external, hybrid).

**Influence:**
- Supplier management focus
- Vendor management objectives
- Relationship management

**Example:**  
Heavy outsourcing → Strong supplier management focus.

---

### 9. IT Implementation Methods

**Description:**  
How IT implements solutions (waterfall, agile, DevOps, etc.).

**Influence:**
- Implementation objectives
- Development practices
- Change management approach

**Example:**  
Agile/DevOps → Focus on agile-friendly objectives.

---

### 10. Technology Adoption Strategy

**Description:**  
How the organization adopts new technologies.

**Influence:**
- Innovation objectives
- Technology management
- Change management

**Example:**  
Early adopter → Focus on innovation objectives.

---

### 11. Enterprise Size

**Description:**  
Size of the organization (small, medium, large).

**Influence:**
- Scope of implementation
- Resource requirements
- Complexity

**Example:**  
Small enterprise → Simplified approach, focus on essential objectives.

---

## Using Design Factors

### Step 1: Assess Design Factors

Assess each design factor for your organization:
- What is your enterprise strategy?
- What are your enterprise goals?
- What is your risk profile?
- etc.

### Step 2: Determine Influence

Determine how each factor influences:
- Which objectives are most important
- Priority of objectives
- Implementation approach

### Step 3: Tailor Framework

Tailor COBIT based on design factors:
- Focus on relevant objectives
- Prioritize implementation
- Customize practices
