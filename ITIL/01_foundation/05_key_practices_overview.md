## What are Practices?

**Practices** are sets of organizational resources designed for performing work or accomplishing an objective.

### Key Characteristics

- **Capabilities:** Provide capabilities for service management
- **Resources:** Include people, processes, technology, information
- **Flexible:** Can be adapted to context
- **Integrated:** Work together to create value

---

## The 34 Practices

ITIL 4 defines **34 practices** organized into three categories:

1. **General Management Practices** (14)
2. **Service Management Practices** (17)
3. **Technical Management Practices** (3)

## Foundation Level Key Practices

For ITIL 4 Foundation, focus on understanding these key practices:

### 1. Incident Management

**Purpose:** To minimize the negative impact of incidents by restoring normal service operation as quickly as possible.

**Key Concepts:**
- Incident lifecycle
- Priority (Impact Ã— Urgency)
- Escalation
- Service restoration

### 2. Problem Management

**Purpose:** To reduce the likelihood and impact of incidents by identifying actual and potential causes of incidents.

**Key Concepts:**
- Problem vs Incident
- Root cause analysis
- Known errors
- Workarounds

### 3. Change Enablement

**Purpose:** To maximize the number of successful IT changes by ensuring that risks have been properly assessed.

**Key Concepts:**
- Change types (Normal, Standard, Emergency)
- Change Advisory Board (CAB)
- Change lifecycle
- Risk assessment

### 4. Service Level Management

**Purpose:** To set clear business-based targets for service performance.

**Key Concepts:**
- Service Level Agreement (SLA)
- Service Level Target
- Service Level Requirements (SLR)

### 5. Service Request Management

**Purpose:** To support the agreed quality of a service by handling all pre-defined, user-initiated service requests.

**Key Concepts:**
- Service requests
- Request fulfillment
- Standard requests

### 6. Continual Improvement

**Purpose:** To align the organization's practices and services with changing business needs.

**Key Concepts:**
- Improvement opportunities
- Measurement
- Improvement initiatives

## Practice Relationships

### Practices Work Together

Practices are not isolated - they work together:

- **Incident Management** works with **Problem Management**
- **Change Enablement** works with **Release Management**
- **Service Level Management** works with **Supplier Management**
- **Continual Improvement** applies to all practices

### Example: Incident Resolution

1. **Incident Management:** Restore service quickly
2. **Problem Management:** Find root cause
3. **Change Enablement:** Implement fix
4. **Release Management:** Deploy fix
5. **Continual Improvement:** Learn and improve
