# General Management Practices

**Level:** Practices  
**Framework:** ITIL 4  
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Overview

ITIL 4 defines **14 General Management Practices** that are used across the organization, not just IT.

---

## The 14 General Management Practices

### 1. Architecture Management
Managing the architecture of the organization.

### 2. Continual Improvement
Continuously improving services and practices.

### 3. Information Security Management
Managing information security.

### 4. Knowledge Management
Managing knowledge and information.

### 5. Measurement and Reporting
Measuring and reporting on performance.

### 6. Organizational Change Management
Managing organizational change.

### 7. Portfolio Management
Managing the service portfolio.

### 8. Project Management
Managing projects.

### 9. Relationship Management
Managing relationships with stakeholders.

### 10. Risk Management
Managing risks.

### 11. Service Financial Management
Managing IT finances.

### 12. Strategy Management
Managing strategy.

### 13. Supplier Management
Managing suppliers.

### 14. Workforce and Talent Management
Managing workforce and talent.

---

## Key Practices for ITSM

### Continual Improvement
Essential for all service management activities.

### Measurement and Reporting
Critical for understanding performance and value.

### Risk Management
Important for managing service risks.

### Relationship Management
Key for stakeholder engagement.
