## Assessment Methodology

### Methodology Selection

#### 1. Self-Assessment

**Description:** Internal assessment by organization.

**Advantages:**
- Cost-effective
- Internal control
- Quick turnaround
- Regular assessment

**Disadvantages:**
- Potential bias
- Limited expertise
- May miss issues
- Less objective

**When to Use:**
- Regular assessments
- Quick assessments
- Limited budget
- Internal capability

#### 2. External Assessment

**Description:** Assessment by external consultants.

**Advantages:**
- Objective assessment
- Expert knowledge
- Benchmarking
- Detailed findings

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher cost
- Time required
- External dependency
- May be disruptive

**When to Use:**
- Comprehensive assessment
- Need for objectivity
- Expert knowledge needed
- Benchmarking required

#### 3. Hybrid Assessment

**Description:** Combination of self and external assessment.

**Advantages:**
- Best of both approaches
- Cost-effective
- Objective validation
- Comprehensive

**Disadvantages:**
- Coordination required
- May be complex
- Time required
- Resource intensive

**When to Use:**
- Comprehensive assessment
- Need for validation
- Budget constraints
- Internal capability exists

## Risk Assessment

### Risk Identification

#### Risk 1: Scope Creep

**Risk:** Assessment scope expands beyond plan.

**Impact:** Delays, cost overruns, resource issues

**Mitigation:**
- Clear scope definition
- Scope change process
- Regular reviews
- Stakeholder alignment

#### Risk 2: Stakeholder Availability

**Risk:** Key stakeholders unavailable.

**Impact:** Delays, incomplete data, limited insights

**Mitigation:**
- Early scheduling
- Flexible scheduling
- Alternative methods
- Clear communication

#### Risk 3: Data Quality Issues

**Risk:** Poor quality or incomplete data.

**Impact:** Inaccurate findings, poor recommendations

**Mitigation:**
- Multiple data sources
- Data validation
- Quality checks
- Expert review

#### Risk 4: Resistance to Assessment

**Risk:** Stakeholders resist assessment.

**Impact:** Limited cooperation, incomplete data

**Mitigation:**
- Clear communication
- Value demonstration
- Stakeholder engagement
- Change management

#### Risk 5: Resource Constraints

**Risk:** Insufficient resources.

**Impact:** Delays, incomplete assessment, poor quality

**Mitigation:**
- Resource planning
- Prioritization
- Efficient methods
- External support

### Risk Management

**Risk Management Process:**
1. Identify risks
2. Assess risks
3. Develop mitigation
4. Monitor risks
5. Respond to risks
