## Overview

This guide helps you prepare for the ITAM Foundation certification exam. It covers exam structure, key topics, and preparation strategies.

---

## Exam Structure

### General Information

- **Exam Type:** Multiple choice
- **Number of Questions:** 60-75 questions
- **Duration:** 60-90 minutes
- **Passing Score:** 65% (typically)
- **Format:** Computer-based or paper-based
- **Language:** English (and other languages may be available)

### Question Types

- **Single Answer:** Choose one correct answer
- **Multiple Choice:** Select all correct answers
- **Scenario-Based:** Questions based on real-world scenarios
- **Definition-Based:** Questions about terminology and concepts

## Practice Questions

### Question 1: ITAM Definition

**What is the primary focus of IT Asset Management?**

a) Service delivery  
b) Financial and lifecycle management  
c) Configuration management  
d) Incident management

**Answer:** b) Financial and lifecycle management

**Explanation:** ITAM focuses on managing assets for financial and lifecycle purposes, while ITIL CM focuses on service delivery.

---

### Question 2: Asset Lifecycle

**What are the five stages of the asset lifecycle?**

a) Plan, Buy, Install, Use, Throw  
b) Plan, Acquire, Deploy, Manage, Retire  
c) Design, Build, Test, Deploy, Monitor  
d) Request, Approve, Purchase, Install, Support

**Answer:** b) Plan, Acquire, Deploy, Manage, Retire

**Explanation:** The five stages are Plan, Acquire, Deploy, Manage, and Retire.

---

### Question 3: Asset vs CI

**What is the difference between an asset and a configuration item?**

a) No difference  
b) Assets are financial, CIs are service-focused  
c) CIs are financial, assets are service-focused  
d) Assets are hardware, CIs are software

**Answer:** b) Assets are financial, CIs are service-focused

**Explanation:** Assets are managed for financial and lifecycle purposes, while CIs are managed to support service delivery.

---

### Question 4: Compliance

**Why is license compliance important in ITAM?**

a) It's not important  
b) To avoid violations and penalties  
c) To improve performance  
d) To reduce costs only

**Answer:** b) To avoid violations and penalties

**Explanation:** License compliance is critical to avoid violations, penalties, and legal issues.

---

### Question 5: TCO

**What does TCO stand for and what does it include?**

a) Total Cost of Ownership - purchase price only  
b) Total Cost of Ownership - all lifecycle costs  
c) Total Cost of Operation - operating costs only  
d) Total Cost Optimization - optimization costs

**Answer:** b) Total Cost of Ownership - all lifecycle costs

**Explanation:** TCO includes all costs over the asset lifecycle, not just purchase price.
