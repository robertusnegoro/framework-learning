## Core ITAM Terms

### Asset

**Definition:** An IT asset is any hardware, software, or cloud resource that has financial value and is managed throughout its lifecycle.

**Key Points:**
- Has financial value
- Managed throughout lifecycle
- Tracked in asset database
- Subject to compliance requirements

**Example:** A server, a software license, a cloud subscription

---

### IT Asset Management (ITAM)

**Definition:** The business practice of managing IT assets throughout their lifecycle to maximize value, minimize costs, and ensure compliance.

**Key Points:**
- Business practice, not just technology
- Lifecycle-focused
- Cost and compliance oriented
- Integrated with IT operations

---

### Asset Lifecycle

**Definition:** The complete journey of an IT asset from planning through retirement.

**Stages:**
- Plan
- Acquire
- Deploy
- Manage
- Retire

---

### Asset Discovery

**Definition:** The process of identifying and cataloging IT assets in an organization.

**Methods:**
- Automated discovery
- Manual discovery
- Hybrid approach

---

### Asset Inventory

**Definition:** A comprehensive record of all IT assets in an organization.

**Components:**
- Asset details
- Location information
- Ownership information
- Financial information

---

### Configuration Item (CI)

**Definition:** A component that needs to be managed to deliver an IT service.

**Key Points:**
- Service-focused
- Managed in CMDB
- Supports service delivery
- May or may not be an asset

**Relationship to Assets:**
- Assets can be CIs
- Not all CIs are assets
- Overlap exists but purposes differ

---

### CMDB (Configuration Management Database)

**Definition:** A database that stores information about configuration items and their relationships.

**Purpose:**
- Support service delivery
- Track service components
- Manage relationships
- Support ITIL processes

**Difference from Asset Database:**
- CMDB: Service-focused
- Asset Database: Financial and compliance-focused

---

## Asset vs Configuration Item

### Asset

- **Focus:** Financial and lifecycle
- **Purpose:** Cost optimization, compliance
- **Scope:** All IT assets
- **Database:** Asset Database
- **Lifecycle:** Plan → Acquire → Deploy → Manage → Retire

### Configuration Item

- **Focus:** Service delivery
- **Purpose:** Support IT services
- **Scope:** Service-related components
- **Database:** CMDB
- **Lifecycle:** Service lifecycle

### Overlap

- Assets can be CIs
- CIs can be assets
- Integration is common
- Shared data improves accuracy

## ISO 19770 Terminology

### Software Asset Management (SAM)

**Definition:** The practice of managing software assets throughout their lifecycle.

**Key Points:**
- Focus on software
- License compliance
- Cost optimization
- ISO 19770 standard

---

### Software Identification Tag (SWID Tag)

**Definition:** A standardized tag that identifies software products and their relationships.

**Purpose:**
- Identify software
- Track installations
- Support compliance
- Enable automation

---

### Software Tag

**Definition:** Metadata that identifies and describes software.

**Types:**
- Primary tags
- Patch tags
- Corpus tags
- Product tags

---

### License Entitlement

**Definition:** The right to use software under specific terms and conditions.

**Key Points:**
- Defined in license agreement
- May have restrictions
- Subject to compliance
- Tracked in SAM

---

### License Compliance

**Definition:** The state of being in compliance with software license agreements.

**Key Points:**
- Avoid violations
- Prevent penalties
- Regular monitoring
- Audit preparation

---

## Financial Terms

### Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

**Definition:** The total cost of an asset over its complete lifecycle.

**Components:**
- Acquisition cost
- Operating costs
- Maintenance costs
- Retirement costs

---

### Return on Investment (ROI)

**Definition:** A measure of the return on an IT investment.

**Calculation:**
- (Gain - Cost) / Cost × 100
- Measures value delivered
- Supports decision-making

---

### Depreciation

**Definition:** The reduction in value of an asset over time.

**Methods:**
- Straight-line
- Accelerated
- Units of production

---

### Cost Allocation

**Definition:** The process of assigning costs to departments, projects, or cost centers.

**Purpose:**
- Budget management
- Chargeback
- Cost visibility
- Financial reporting

---

## Discovery Terms

### Automated Discovery

**Definition:** The use of tools to automatically identify and catalog IT assets.

**Methods:**
- Network scanning
- Agent-based discovery
- API-based discovery
- Cloud discovery

---

### Manual Discovery

**Definition:** The process of manually identifying and cataloging IT assets.

**Methods:**
- Physical inventory
- Documentation review
- Interviews
- Surveys

---

### Reconciliation

**Definition:** The process of comparing and aligning data from multiple sources.

**Purpose:**
- Ensure accuracy
- Identify discrepancies
- Resolve conflicts
- Maintain data quality

---

## Compliance Terms

### License Audit

**Definition:** A review of software licenses to ensure compliance.

**Types:**
- Vendor audits
- Internal audits
- Third-party audits

---

### License Violation

**Definition:** The use of software in a way that violates license terms.

**Consequences:**
- Financial penalties
- Legal issues
- Reputation damage
- Business disruption

---

### Regulatory Compliance

**Definition:** Adherence to industry and government regulations.

**Examples:**
- Data protection regulations
- Industry standards
- Government requirements
