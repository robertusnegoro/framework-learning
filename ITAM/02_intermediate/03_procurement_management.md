## Overview

Procurement management in ITAM involves managing the acquisition of IT assets from planning through contract execution. Effective procurement management ensures optimal value, compliance, and lifecycle planning.

---

## Procurement Lifecycle

The procurement lifecycle consists of **6 main stages**:

1. **Plan** - Requirements and planning
2. **Source** - Vendor selection
3. **Negotiate** - Contract negotiation
4. **Purchase** - Order placement
5. **Receive** - Asset receipt
6. **Manage** - Contract and vendor management

---

## Stage 1: Plan

### Purpose

Define requirements and plan procurement.

### Key Activities

- **Requirements Definition**
  - Identify business needs
  - Define technical requirements
  - Assess current assets
  - Plan for future needs

- **Budget Planning**
  - Estimate costs
  - Allocate budget
  - Plan for lifecycle costs
  - Consider TCO

- **Procurement Planning**
  - Define procurement approach
  - Plan timeline
  - Identify stakeholders
  - Plan approvals

### Deliverables

- Requirements document
- Budget plan
- Procurement plan
- Approval documentation

## Stage 2: Source

### Purpose

Identify and select vendors.

### Key Activities

- **Vendor Research**
  - Research potential vendors
  - Evaluate options
  - Compare solutions
  - Assess capabilities

- **Vendor Evaluation**
  - Evaluate vendors
  - Assess fit
  - Check references
  - Review financials

- **Vendor Selection**
  - Compare options

## Purchase Approval Workflows

### Approval Levels

**Level 1: Standard Purchases**
- Low value purchases
- Standard items
- Pre-approved vendors
- Fast approval

**Level 2: Important Purchases**
- Medium value purchases
- Non-standard items
- Department approval
- Moderate approval time

**Level 3: Strategic Purchases**
- High value purchases
- Strategic items
- Executive approval
- Extended approval time

### Approval Process

1. **Request Submission**
   - Submit purchase request
   - Include justification
   - Attach documentation
   - Route for approval

2. **Approval Review**
   - Review request
   - Evaluate need
   - Check budget
   - Make decision

3. **Approval Decision**
   - Approve or reject
   - Add conditions
   - Document decision
   - Notify requester

4. **Purchase Execution**
   - Create purchase order
   - Place order
   - Track order
   - Receive asset

### Approval Best Practices

- **Clear Criteria:** Define approval criteria
- **Efficient Process:** Streamline approval process
- **Documentation:** Document all approvals
- **Audit Trail:** Maintain audit trail
- **Timely Decisions:** Make timely decisions
