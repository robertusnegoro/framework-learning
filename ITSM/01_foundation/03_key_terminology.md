# ITSM Key Terminology

**Level:** Foundation  
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this document, you should understand:
- Essential ITSM terms and definitions
- How terms relate to each other
- Common terminology used in service management
- Differences between similar terms

---

## Core Service Terms

### Service
A means of delivering value to customers by facilitating outcomes customers want to achieve without the ownership of specific costs and risks.

**Example:** Email service, CRM service, network service

### Service Management
A set of specialized organizational capabilities for providing value to customers in the form of services.

### IT Service Management (ITSM)
The implementation and management of quality IT services that meet the needs of the business.

### Service Provider
An organization that provides services to customers. Can be:
- **Type I:** Internal (same organization)
- **Type II:** Shared services (same parent company)
- **Type III:** External (separate organization)

---

## Value and Outcomes

### Value
The perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance of something.

**Formula:** Value = Outcomes - Costs - Risks

### Outcome
A result that customers want to achieve.

**Example:** Employees can communicate effectively (outcome of email service)

### Output
A tangible or intangible deliverable of an activity.

**Example:** Email sent (output) â†’ Communication achieved (outcome)

### Utility
The functionality offered by a product or service to meet a particular need.

**Example:** Email service provides communication functionality (utility)

### Warranty
Assurance that a product or service will meet agreed requirements.

**Example:** Email service available 99.9% of the time (warranty)

---

## Stakeholders

### Customer
A person who defines requirements and takes responsibility for outcomes.

**Example:** Head of Sales (defines CRM requirements)

### User
A person who uses the service on a day-to-day basis.

**Example:** Sales representatives (use CRM system)

### Service Owner
A person accountable for the delivery of a service.

### Process Owner
A person accountable for a process.

### Supplier
A third-party organization that provides components or services.

**Example:** Cloud provider, software vendor

---

## Service Level Terms

### Service Level Agreement (SLA)
An agreement between service provider and customer about service levels.

**Example:** Email service available 99.9% of the time, response time < 2 seconds

### Operational Level Agreement (OLA)
An agreement between internal IT groups.

**Example:** Service Desk agrees to respond to incidents within 15 minutes

### Underpinning Contract (UC)
A contract with external suppliers.

**Example:** Contract with cloud provider for infrastructure

### Service Level Target
A specific, measurable target for service performance.

**Example:** 99.9% availability, < 2 second response time

---

## Incident and Problem Terms

### Incident
An unplanned interruption to a service or reduction in quality.

**Example:** Email service is down, printer not working

### Problem
The cause of one or more incidents.

**Example:** Root cause of email outages is database corruption

### Known Error
A problem that has been analyzed but not yet resolved.

**Example:** Database corruption identified, fix being developed

### Workaround
A temporary solution to restore service while problem is being fixed.

**Example:** Use backup email server while main server is fixed

---

## Change Terms

### Change
The addition, modification, or removal of anything that could affect IT services.

**Example:** Upgrading email server, deploying new application

### Change Request (RFC)
A formal request for a change.

### Change Advisory Board (CAB)
A group that approves changes.

### Emergency Change
A change that must be implemented urgently.

**Example:** Security patch for critical vulnerability

### Standard Change
A pre-approved change that follows a standard procedure.

**Example:** Password reset, account creation

### Normal Change
A change that requires approval through standard process.

---

## Configuration Terms

### Configuration Item (CI)
Any component that needs to be managed to deliver an IT service.

**Example:** Server, application, network device, document

### Configuration Management Database (CMDB)
A database containing information about CIs and their relationships.

### Asset
Any resource or capability that has value to an organization.

**Example:** Hardware, software, people, information

### Asset Management
The process responsible for tracking and reporting the value and ownership of assets.

---

## Release Terms

### Release
A collection of changes deployed together.

**Example:** Version 2.0 of an application

### Release Package
A collection of components deployed together.

### Deployment
The activity of installing, configuring, and activating a release.

### Build
The activity of assembling components into a release.

---

## Service Catalog Terms

### Service Catalog
A database or structured document with information about all services.

### Service Portfolio
The complete set of services managed by a service provider.

**Includes:**
- **Service Pipeline:** Services in development
- **Service Catalog:** Live services
- **Retired Services:** Services no longer offered

---

## Availability Terms

### Availability
The ability of a service to perform its required function when needed.

**Formula:** Availability = (Agreed Service Time - Downtime) / Agreed Service Time

### Reliability
The ability of a service to perform consistently.

### Maintainability
The ability of a service to be maintained or restored.

### Serviceability
The ability of a supplier to meet service level requirements.

---

## Capacity Terms

### Capacity
The maximum throughput a service can deliver.

**Example:** Email service can handle 10,000 emails per hour

### Capacity Management
The process of ensuring adequate capacity to meet demand.

### Demand Management
The process of understanding and influencing customer demand.

---

## Security Terms

### Information Security
Protection of information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, etc.

### Information Security Management
The process of managing information security.

### Vulnerability
A weakness that could be exploited.

**Example:** Unpatched software, weak password policy

### Threat
A potential cause of an incident.

**Example:** Malware, hackers, natural disasters

### Risk
The probability of a threat exploiting a vulnerability.

---

## Financial Terms

### Cost
The amount of money spent on something.

**Types:**
- **Capital Expenditure (CapEx):** One-time costs (hardware)
- **Operational Expenditure (OpEx):** Ongoing costs (licenses, support)

### Chargeback
Charging customers for services they use.

### Showback
Showing customers what services cost without charging.

### Budget
A financial plan for a period of time.

---

## Process Terms

### Process
A structured set of activities designed to accomplish a specific objective.

**Characteristics:**
- Measurable
- Produces specific outputs
- Delivers outcomes
- Responds to triggers

### Function
A team or group of people with specific responsibilities.

**Example:** Service Desk, Technical Management

### Procedure
A documented way to carry out an activity or process.

### Work Instruction
A detailed step-by-step guide for performing a task.

---

## Knowledge Terms

### Knowledge Management
The process of creating, sharing, using, and managing knowledge.

### Knowledge Base
A database of knowledge articles.

### Known Error Database (KEDB)
A database of known errors and workarounds.

### Service Knowledge Management System (SKMS)
A set of tools and databases for managing knowledge.

---

## Measurement Terms

### Metric
A measurement used to assess performance.

**Example:** Number of incidents, resolution time

### Key Performance Indicator (KPI)
A metric used to measure success.

**Example:** 95% of incidents resolved within SLA

### Critical Success Factor (CSF)
Something that must happen for success.

**Example:** Service availability, customer satisfaction

### Baseline
A starting point for measurement.

---

## Service Desk Terms

### Service Desk
The single point of contact between service provider and users.

**Types:**
- **Call Center:** Handles high volume of calls
- **Help Desk:** Focuses on fixing issues
- **Service Desk:** Full service management

### First Line Support
Initial support level (Service Desk).

### Second Line Support
Specialist support (Technical teams).

### Third Line Support
Expert support (Vendors, developers).

### Escalation
Moving an incident to a higher support level.

## Key Differences

### Incident vs Problem
- **Incident:** Symptom (service is down)
- **Problem:** Cause (root cause of downtime)

### Process vs Function
- **Process:** What to do (activities)
- **Function:** Who does it (people/teams)

### Output vs Outcome
- **Output:** What is produced (email sent)
- **Outcome:** Result achieved (communication)

### Utility vs Warranty
- **Utility:** What it does (functionality)
- **Warranty:** How well it does it (quality)

### Service vs Product
- **Service:** Intangible, consumed when delivered
- **Product:** Tangible, can be stored
